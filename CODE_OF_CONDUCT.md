# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

ThreatForge is a community built around learning, collaboration, and the belief that security knowledge should be accessible to everyone.

We are committed to maintaining an environment where every person — regardless of age, background, experience level, gender identity, nationality, race, religion, sexual orientation, or disability — can participate with dignity and without fear of harassment or exclusion.

This Code of Conduct describes the standards we hold ourselves to and the responsibilities we share as a community.

---

## Our Standards

### What we expect

- Communicating with honesty and respect
- Offering feedback that is specific, constructive, and kind
- Being patient with people who are learning — everyone starts somewhere
- Accepting that others may approach problems differently than you would
- Crediting others for their ideas and work
- Prioritizing what's best for the community over individual preference

### What we won't tolerate

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks in any form
- Discriminatory language or behavior based on identity or background
- Deliberate misgendering or use of rejected names
- Sharing someone's private information without their consent
- Dismissing contributions because of who submitted them, not what they contain
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or community spaces
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all ThreatForge spaces — the GitHub repository, pull requests, issues, discussions, and any other community channels associated with the project.

It also applies when an individual is representing ThreatForge publicly — for example, at events, in official social media posts, or in public statements made on behalf of the project.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by opening a private issue or reaching out to a maintainer directly.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion. The identity of the person reporting will be kept confidential.

We take every report seriously. No one should have to tolerate harmful behavior in order to contribute to this project.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards.

Depending on the nature and severity of the violation, responses may include:

- A private message clarifying the expected behavior
- A public or private warning
- Temporary or permanent removal from the community

Maintainers who fail to uphold this Code of Conduct in good faith may face consequences determined by the project leadership.
